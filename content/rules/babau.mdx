# Babau

[![](http://localhost:8200/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/babau-706x1024.jpg)](http://localhost:8200/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/babau.jpg)

Art by Michael Bielaczyc

Babau’s are often used as soldiers by more powerful entities in the Navirim. They are the perfect soldiers: tough, immune to most damage, capable of multiple powerful attacks. During the Great War, the Uthgard Kingdom had a legion of babau, controlled by a powerful Dreadlord mage.

When a babau finds itself alone in our world, they often stalk a victim, relishing in its fear and distress. This has lead to a folk tale view of them as boogeymen, hiding under beds or in closets to torment their victims. In truth, if a babau stalks a person, that person will not likely last long.

_—_Oharad_, Cryptonombus of the Navirim_

**Protective Slime**

A layer of acidic slime coats a babau’s skin. Any creature that strikes a babau with a natural attack or unarmed strike takes 1d8 points of acid damage from this slime if it fails an Agility save. A creature that strikes a babau with a melee weapon must make an Agility save, or the weapon takes 1d8 points of acid damage. Ammunition that strikes a babau is automatically destroyed after it inflicts its damage.

**Sneak Attack**

The babau does an extra 2d6 points of damage whenever a foe is unaware of them, flanked, or prone.
