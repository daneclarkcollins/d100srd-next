# Basilisk

[![](http://localhost:8200/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/basilisk-805x1024.jpg)](http://localhost:8200/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/basilisk.jpg)

Art by Michael Bielaczyc

A basilisk is a giant reptile that can turn flesh and blood creature to stone. They can grow up to 12 feet long from snout to tip of tail, though they have short legs and, other than their stony gaze, can only bite their prey.

It is thought that they were created by ancient wizard magic for guarding of places of power.

_—_Wolfram Buhl_, Buc de Megichen Teir_, (translated to the common tongue in 5763)

**CV:** 4

**Class:** Monster

**Type:** Magical Beast

**Speed:** 30’

**HP:** 37

**Damage Modifier:** 1d6

**Armor Value:** 5

**Attack:** Bite\* 75% 1d6+4+1d6

**Skills:** Dodge 50%, Jump 40%, Climb 40%, Spot 40%, Listen 40%, Hide 24%, Stealth 24% 

**Special:** Stone Breath

**Weaknesses:** Mirrors

**Horror:** (If Turning to Stone) 2/2d4

**Treasure:** Standard (Lair)

**\*Bite** The large mouth of a basilisk can only be dodged or parried with a shield; weapons are too small to block it.

**Horror** Anyone who begins to turn to stone suffers Horror at the unnatural transformation.

**Stone Breath** Contrary to belief, it is not the gaze of a basilisk that turns people to stone; it is its breath. Once every two rounds, as a Move or Standard action, a basilisk can breathe a 15’ cone of breath that can turn the skin of living creatures and plants into stone. A _difficult_ Stamina check can resist the monster's breath. If it is not resisted, the skin of the affected creature or plant begins to calcify, turning to a gray stone-like surface. In the first 2 rounds, the affected creature can only move at half speed. In the next two rounds, they can no longer walk, but they can complete other actions, but any action is difficult. In the following two rounds, they can speak but nothing more. They become encased in the calci-stone, alive but unable to move. 

A creature petrified in this matter that is then coated (not just splashed) with fresh basilisk saliva (taken from a basilisk no more than 1 hour dead) is instantly restored to flesh. A single basilisk contains enough saliva to coat 1d3 Medium creatures in this manner.

**Treasure** A basilisk enjoys hoarding belongings from those it hunts. It likes shiny objects piling them in the back of its lair.

Average worth 600 gp. 50% coins, 30% gems and jewelry, 20% objects and equipment.

### Reagents and Remnants

A basilisk skin makes a hardy cloak, able to withstand most elements and gains a +20% to rolls vs acid. They have 3 doses of saliva that can dissolve stone (a 6’x6’x1’ section reach). And they have a gland that contains a liquid that acts the same as their Stone Breath. Removing this gland is delicate, and any mishap on a Survival check means the harvester becomes contaminated with the glands contents and begins to turn.
