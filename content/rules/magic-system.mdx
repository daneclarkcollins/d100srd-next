# Magic System

Magic in SagaBorn D100 is powered by **Mana** and uses a freeform spellcasting system that emphasizes narrative storytelling and tactical decision-making.

## Mana

**Mana** is the magical energy that powers spells. Every character has a **Spirit Points** pool equal to their **ACU × 5**, which represents their mana capacity.

### Mana Regeneration

- **Full Rest (8 hours):** Recover all spent mana
- **Short Rest (1 hour):** Recover ACU in mana points
- **Meditation:** Some talents allow faster mana recovery

## Spellcasting Basics

### Casting a Spell

1. **Declare the Spell:** State what spell you're casting and how much mana you're spending
2. **Make the Roll:** Roll 1d100 against your **Magic** skill
3. **Spend Mana:** Mana is spent whether the spell succeeds or fails
4. **Apply Effects:** On success, the spell takes effect as described

### Spell Success Levels

- **Critical Success (01-05):** Maximum effect, potential bonus
- **Success:** Normal spell effect
- **Failure:** Spell fails, mana still spent
- **Fumble (96-00):** Spell fails with potential negative consequences

## Spell Categories

### Damage Spells

These spells directly harm targets through various means.

**Examples:** Acid Bolt, Elemental Arrow, Lightning Strike

**Targeting:** Use **Spot** skill for ranged spell attacks instead of Ranged Weapons skill

### Area of Effect (AoE) Spells  

Spells that affect multiple targets or a large area.

**Examples:** Acid Fog, Fireball, Ice Storm

**Targeting:** Use **Spot** skill to aim at a specific space. Miss results scatter the effect.

### Buff Spells

Spells that enhance the target's abilities or provide beneficial effects.

**Examples:** Aid, Bear's Strength, Cat's Dexterity

**Duration:** Most buffs last for a specific duration or until triggered

### Utility Spells

Spells that provide non-combat benefits and problem-solving capabilities.

**Examples:** Animal Friend, Countermagic, Detect Magic

## Ranged Damage Spells - Aiming

Some spells, such as **Acid Bolt** or **Elemental Arrow**, are treated as ranged attacks. However, a mage uses the **Spot** skill to aim the spell instead of the **Ranged Weapons** skill.

## Area of Effect Spells

### Targeting AoE Spells

When attempting to hit a specific space or target with an Area of Effect (AoE) spell, the caster must aim with the **Spot** skill. The mage must choose a target space and make a **Spot** skill check to hit it accurately.

#### Scatter Rules

If the **Spot** check fails when targeting an AoE spell:

1. **Roll 1d8** to determine scatter direction (1=North, 2=NE, etc.)
2. **Roll 1d10** to determine distance in feet the spell scatters
3. The spell effect occurs at the scattered location instead

## Spell Resistance and Avoidance

### Avoidance Types

Different spells allow different types of avoidance:

- **Dodge:** Physical avoidance of the spell effect
- **Parry:** Blocking or deflecting the magical energy  
- **Spirit:** Mental resistance against mind-affecting magic
- **None:** No avoidance possible

### Spell Resistance

Some creatures and magical items provide **Spell Resistance**, which reduces incoming magical damage or provides bonuses to avoidance rolls.

## Counterspells and Dispelling

### Countermagic

The **Countermagic** spell can be used to:
- Interrupt a spell being cast (if you act before the caster)
- Dispel an ongoing magical effect
- Provide resistance against incoming spells

### Dispel Magic

Permanent magical effects, enchanted items, and persistent spells can often be dispelled through specific spells or abilities.

## Spell Components

### Verbal Components

Most spells require spoken incantations. Characters who are **silenced** or **gagged** cannot cast spells with verbal components.

### Somatic Components  

Spells require precise hand movements and gestures. Characters who are **bound** or **grappled** may have difficulty casting spells.

### Material Components

Some powerful spells may require specific material components, rare reagents, or focus items.

## Learning New Spells

### Spell Research

Characters can research and develop new spells by:
- Studying ancient tomes and magical texts
- Experimenting with magical theory
- Learning from other spellcasters
- Discovering spell knowledge through adventure

### Spell Limitations

- **Mana Cost:** More powerful effects require more mana
- **Skill Requirements:** Complex spells may require higher Magic skill
- **Knowledge:** You must know a spell to cast it

## Magical Mishaps

When a spellcaster **fumbles** (rolls 96-00), consult the following table:

| Roll 1d6 | Mishap Effect |
|----------|---------------|
| 1-2 | **Spell Surge:** Spell works but affects random nearby target |
| 3-4 | **Magical Feedback:** Caster takes 1d4 damage |
| 5-6 | **Mana Drain:** Lose an additional 1d4 mana points |

## Advanced Magic Rules

### Overcharging Spells

Experienced mages can spend additional mana beyond a spell's normal cost to enhance its effects:
- **+50% Mana:** +25% effect (damage, duration, etc.)
- **+100% Mana:** +50% effect
- **Risk:** Each level of overcharging increases fumble range by 5

### Ritual Magic

Powerful spells can be cast as rituals:
- **Casting Time:** 10 minutes to several hours
- **Benefits:** Reduced mana cost, enhanced effects
- **Requirements:** Specific components, safe environment

### Group Casting

Multiple spellcasters can combine their efforts:
- **Lead Caster:** Makes the Magic skill roll
- **Assistants:** Contribute mana to the spell
- **Risk:** Failure affects all participants
