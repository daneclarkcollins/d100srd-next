Dryads have been part of Atheles for as long as humans or elves have kept records, and the dwarves say they have always been here. Dryads, themselves, have kept no written history, nor do they seem to have any interest discussing their past, so it is doubtful we will ever know where they originated.

They are rare to find, and you will only encounter them when they choose for you to. They have the appearance of a short elf: wide, large eyes, pointed ears, and slim features. Their skin is dark gray-brown, and their hair changes with the colors of the leaves.

Shy, intelligent, and caring, they never prefer violence, unless someone or something is threatening their forest or tree.

— Etahn

- **Habitat:** Any Forest

**Magic Abilities**

- **At Will:** Slow - The target becomes slowed. A slowed creature moves at 1/2 speed
- **3/day:** Befriend - The target sees the spellcaster as a friend.
- **1/day:** Sleep - Target up to 7 creatures, is they fail an Acumen roll, they fall asleep for 1 minute.

**Speak With Plants**
A dryad can converse with plants in a limited fashion. Plants can give vague impressions of what they have sensed.

**Tree-Dependent**
Dryads must stay within 300 yards of their bonded tree or begin to suffer illness and death in 4d6 hours.

**Tree Shape**
Can assume the form of a tree or shrub. Only a see magic spell reveals the transmutation aura.

**Tree Stride**
Can move between trees that are within line of sight.

**Treasure**
Dryads love gold and jewels, and will have a small stash near their tree. They will also look kindly on those who bring gifts.
