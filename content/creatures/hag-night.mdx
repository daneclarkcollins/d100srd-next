Stalking the night hag remains my greatest shame as a hunter.

Dazareum and Etahn begrudgingly agreed, at my insistence, that we would not only free the hag's coerced minions but that we would capture the creature alive. We had the means, and by bringing it back alive, it would teach us more than we could imagine about Navirites. I was sure could have spent the subsequent years studying nothing else.

But then I saw its minions and everything changed. I was disturbed to my core at seeing these former people come toward us, driven by nothing but hunger for flesh. Severed from their minds and souls, there was nothing left of them to save. When called into the void, a person should have the right to die with dignity; these unfortunates were given no such boon. The best thing we could do for them was to set them free—by destroying them.

So, we dispatched the minions and came to an unspoken agreement. The night hag would die this night. No chance could be taken that she would inflict this wickedness on even one more innocent.

When we reached her, those intentions were cemented when we discovered that her chamber was filled with even more of her zombie minions, along with captives as yet unturned, wailing in abject fear as they awaited their own inevitable demise and reanimation.

Overcome with horror, disgust, and fury, we slaughtered the hag, and I am ashamed to admit that I took a certain grim pleasure in dragging her death out. Come the next day, however, my conscience weighed heavily. We saved many lives that night by taking that creature's life, but had we taken her back to the Tower for study, we might have saved so many more to come, for she was certainly not the last of her kind.

—Delgon

Night hags. Gross creatures. They feed on fear and nightmares. The one we faced had been using a town to try and create an unholy army. I had never seen such a thing, humans turned into vessels of the energy from the Navirim. There was no saving them.

We fought our way down into the witch's inner sanctum. Her blank eyes were set in a pale and withered face, sunken back into her skeletal head. Stringy white hair shot out in all directions, her thin lips parted in a hideous smile of crooked yellow teeth. She was taller than us, well over six feet, and deathly thin, her frame appearing even more slight than the tattered and filthy rags stolen from corpses that she piled on her disgusting body.

As we approached, she battered us with her magic as her minions attacked us, but they could not hold us back. We were there to pay her back for the things she had done to these innocent people. While my arrows had little effect, my blade cut through, and she and her awful reign of terror came to an end.

—Etahn

### Special Abilities

**Dream Haunting**  
Using a Heartstone, the hag can enter the dreams of a sleeping target with mana. Victim suffers 1 CON damage, cannot regain mana from rest, and loses 2 mana (5 if Corruption ≥ 10).

**Disease — Demon Fever**  
Bite; Save- *Difficult* Stamina; onset immediate; 2 CON damage; cure requires two consecutive saves.

**Spell-Like Abilities** *(Mana costs in parentheses)*  
At Will as a standard action — *mage bolt (3)*, *enfeeble (3)*, *sleep* (3).

**Etherealness**  
Can shift into the In-Between for 10 rounds while in possession of Heartstone.

**Heartstone**  
Magical gem (1,800 gp) granting +10% to all saves and immunity to disease. Required for Etherealness. Loses magic 24 hours after hag's death or separation.

**Summon Sisters**  
Through Ritual of the Dream Mark and Pool of Pain (see below), can bring other Navirites into Atheles.

**Ritual of the Dream Mark**  
1-hour ritual cuts symbols into a helpless victim, making them a mindless slave and storing their life essence.

**Pool of Pain**  
Navirite cauldron powered by Dream Mark victims. Gains powers as souls accumulate:

- **1+ souls**: Contact deals 3 CON damage (Fort vs. 65 for half).
- **3+ souls**: 60 ft. *fear* aura (Will vs. 65).
- **5+ souls**: Gains *disconnect* spell, can project consciousness to Navirim.
- **10+ souls**: Create 1-min tear to Navirim (1 creature).
- **20+ souls**: Create 10-min portal to Navirim (3 med. or 1 large creature).

**Covey Leader**  
Always leads a hag covey. Gains *Empower Spell* effect when around their coven (+1d6 to all spell damage dice).
