An iron golem is like other golems, mostly made in the shape of a humanoid, though this one is made from… well, iron. The thing with iron is, it is hard. And we all know iron's negative effect on magic. So these guys are hard to take down. Doesn't mean you can't do it. We knew an iron golem was guarding the lower levels of an ancient Eldar ruin, and those guys really liked their golems, so we had to take it down to explore the ruin fully. Luckily, Delgon found some old dusty tome talking about how tritium is the best way to cut one of these guys down. Forging a tritium blade was not cheap, but the money spent on it was better than that on a coffin.

— Etahn

**Breath Weapon**

An iron golem magically builds up a charge, allowing it to "breathe" a cone of electricity for 15' as a move or standard action. Those caught in it take 3d6 electrical damage, and if they have metal armor, they take an additional 1d6 damage. Dodge for half damage.

**Magic Immunity**

- *Fire*: Slowed for 3 rounds (no save)
- *Electricity*: Heals 1 hp per 3 dmg taken
