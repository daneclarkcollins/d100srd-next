A "daevar" (the elven name for a ravenous corpse) is a long-dead corpse possessed by a spirit from the In-Between. These spirits have spent so long between worlds that their sole desire is to feed when they enter Uteria.

Often they inhabit long dead and skeletal bodies, the common feature being a long-pointed tongue which seems to grow from whatever rotting flesh is left on the body.

They are fast-moving, and have an ability to cause the area around them to seem darker, as if a dark mist forms in the air around it. Its claws and bite cause paralysis in most victims, which allows it to begin to feast on the victim's flesh. It is not particular, with any living creature being a suitable meal for this horrific unnatural hunter.

*—Oharad, Cryptonombus of the Navirim*

**Artful Dodger**

Once per round, a daevar can use a Free Action as a Move Action.

**Blind Fight**

A Ravenous Corpse may reroll one missed attack due to blindness or the dark spell per round.

**Darkness**

The ravenous corpse can extend the shadows around it once per day. This magic like ability creates a sphere of pitch black in a 20’ diameter sphere around the ravenous corpse. It can see perfectly, but all others are effectively blind within the darkness. Mundane lights can not dispel it, but magical lights can illuminate within this darkness.

**Ravenous Hunger**

A creature hit by the Tongue Lash of a daevar must succeed at a CON save or contract ravenous hunger. Once contracted, the diseased creature becomes increasingly hungry, needing 3x the amount of daily sustenance or suffering 1 CON for each day that they can not fulfill their hunger. A diseased creature can cure itself with a *Difficult* Stamina check.

**Paralysis**

Those hit by a ravenous corpse’s tongue attack must succeed on a Stamina save or be paralyzed for 1d4+1 rounds.
