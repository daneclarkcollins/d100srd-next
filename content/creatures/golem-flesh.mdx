A horrific construction of gathered body parts, a flesh golem is a soulless attempt at reanimating life. Most animals run in fear of flesh golems, refusing to attack something so unnatural. They stand around 8' tall and weigh 300 to 500 lbs. They can not speak, but they can let loose a loud moan that can shake you to your core. They do not understand how to use weapons, attacking instead with their bare hands, slamming you with unnatural strength.

— Etahn

**Special Abilities**

**Bellow**
Once per combat as a free action, the flesh golem emits a hollow, booming roar. All creatures within 60 ft. who can hear must succeed on a **Stamina** save or be *Shaken* for 1d4 rounds.

**Berserk**
At the start of each combat round, there is a cumulative 1% chance the golem's elemental spirit breaks free, causing it to attack the nearest living creature or object indiscriminately.

**Fear of Fire**

If the golem is damaged by fire, or is threatened by fire, the chance for it going berserker increases to 10% per round.

**Construct Traits**
Immune to disease, death effects, mind-affecting effects, paralysis, poison, sleep, stun, and any effect requiring a Fortitude save.

**Immunity to Magic**

- Cold: Slows the golem for 2d6 rounds (no save).
- Fire: Does double damage.
- Electricity magic: Cancels any slow effect and heals 1 HP per 3 damage it would cause (excess becomes temporary HP). No save.
