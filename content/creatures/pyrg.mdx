As magic has returned, so have creatures that take the normal nuisances of the world and turn them into nightmarish pests. A pyrg is a giant blood-drinking insect nearly eighteen inches long whose needle-like proboscis can drain a grown teran in only a few minutes.

Although solitary pyrgs are little more than an annoyance, swarms have been known to drive travelers from the road and leave livestock as dried husks.

—Etahn, *Creature Compendium*

**SIZ Note**

Tiny (SIZ 2) = +10% to Dodge, Hide, and Stealth (already applied).

**Attach**

If a pyrg successfully hits with its proboscis, it immediately attempts a Heroic Action to Grapple. If successful, it latches onto the victim using its hooked legs.

While attached, the pyrg loses its DEX bonus to defense, reducing its Dodge to 40%, but remains firmly attached until removed.

Removing a pyrg requires two successful Heroic Actions: one to grab the creature and another to pull it free. A pyrg may only attach to Small or larger creatures.

**Blood Drain**

At the beginning of each of the pyrg's turns while attached, it automatically drains blood, inflicting 1d4 damage.

After draining a total of 10 HP, the pyrg detaches and flies away to digest its meal. If the victim dies before this amount is drained, the pyrg immediately seeks another living target.

**Weapon Dexterity**

The pyrg's hooked legs and agility allow it to use DEX instead of STR whenever making Heroic Actions to Attach or maintain a Grapple.

**Darkvision**

A pyrg can see normally in darkness out to 60 feet, provided there is at least the faintest available light.

**Fragile**

Despite their speed, pyrgs are delicate creatures. Any successful attack that deals damage kills a normal pyrg unless the StoryGuide determines extraordinary circumstances apply.
