**Web**

Many monstrous spiders wait within their webs or in trees above, lowering themselves silently to ambush prey.

A spider can create a web up to eight times per day. It can be a sheet of webbing or shot at a foe up to 50 ft away. Ranged web attacks act as a standard ranged attack. On a success, the target becomes Entangled. Webs can restrain creatures up to double the SIZ of the spider.

An entangled creature may escape with a DEX check or burst free with a STR check, both Difficult.

Spiders also create sheet webs 5–60 ft across to trap prey. Creatures must succeed at a Spot check to notice the web before entering it. Failure results in becoming Entangled.

Web sections can be destroyed, and fire burns away one 5 ft section per round.

Spiders move freely on their own webs and can instantly detect the location of any creature touching them.

## Death's Eye Spider

A giant brown hunting spider found only in the Kaelnor Forest, the death's eye spider derives its name from the ghostly white oval marking its abdomen. Unlike most giant spiders, it does not spin webs to catch prey, instead stalking and ambushing victims before delivering a venomous bite.

Arnach goblins prize these spiders as both mounts and hunting companions. Spider wranglers can coax them into weaving barriers, traps, and tangled mazes through the forest, despite the spiders never using webs to capture prey naturally.

—Etahn, *Creature Compendium*

**Darkvision**

A death's eye spider can see normally in darkness out to 60 feet, provided there is at least the faintest available light.

**Spider Climb**

Death's eye spiders effortlessly climb walls, ceilings, trees, and other rough surfaces at their full movement rate. They never need to make Climb checks except on magically slick or otherwise unnatural surfaces. Their Climb skill is primarily used when resisting Heroic Actions or extraordinary environmental hazards.
