*A small creature looks at you through marble-like eyes. Its skin is an unhealthy pale color of something that sees little sun, and its hair and nails are yellow. Its leather armor smells of dead animals and old dirt. It raises a wickedly notched blade menacingly.*

— *The Heroic Tales of Dayden Mitheris*

Goblins are a varied folk, and they have lived in Athleles for as long as there have been records. The goblin's belief is that they were formed in a dark outer realm by some of the old gods to bring chaos. They are likened to be the almost opposite of elflings, where elfling culture brings joy and song - with the obvious fun chaos, the culture of the goblins is one of violence and aggression, with a nice helping of destructive chaos.

— *Jager, Monster Hunter*

Goblins may be one of the most despised species in Atheles. Elflings, known for their nonviolence and happy demeanor, turn to assault whenever they encounter goblins. Goblins are petty, vengeful, torturing little creatures who enjoy the pain of others. If there is a cause that can hurt others, goblins will flock to it like flies to dung. It is the sages of the towers who debate what made them this way, but for hunters, it is often just part of the job to clean out areas infested with the hateful things.

Many a young adventurer's first contract is to root out the goblins from some new lair, though some of those young explorers often end up as broken skeletons in those same lairs.

— *Etahn*

I am not sure what a goblin has done to Etahn in the past, but his narrow view of these creatures is, sadly, unwavering. While many goblins have a culture that can seem gross to outsiders, not all goblins fall into this category. In Tiren, there are a few goblin settlements that trade regularly with others, and in those lands, you can find goblins traveling between other settlements. Even ancient Uthgard was recorded as having a "goblintown" within the old city of Pardalor. Like other beings, goblins' personalities and beliefs cover a wide gamut, sadly many only encounter those with the sharp notched swords that Etahn is referring to.

— *Delgon*

### Goblin, Arnach

Arnach goblins, or spider goblins, are a strange type of goblin that emerged during the Dark Return from the deep recesses of the In-Between. Even other goblin cultures abhor these goblins. They enjoy eating the flesh of all living beings, especially after they have spent time becoming 'ripe" in their spider's webs.

They worship a dark entity, some sort of shadow deity or elder god, and she brings prosperity when they sacrifice in her name.

**Spider Sac Arrows**

Arnach goblins can use special arrows that replace the standard arrowhead with a large spider sac. Upon a successful ranged attack, these arrows deal normal damage and unleash a swarm of spiderlings upon the target. Brushing off the spiders requires a *Standard Action* to completely remove the spiderlings and sac. Doing so disperses the swarm.

If a character attempts to perform any action prior to wiping off the spiders, the task becomes *difficult*. The spider swarm deals an automatic 1 point of damage to any creature whose space it occupies at the end of their move, with no attack roll needed and bypassing AV.

**Equipment and Treasure**
Rough Hide Armor, Rough Shortbow, Rough Short Spear, 10x arrows, 1d3 spider sac arrow, 2d6 goblin beads, unknown meat jerky, and 1d4 copper coins.
