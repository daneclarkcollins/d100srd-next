A mummy is an undead creature who went through a lengthy embalming process and was then resurrected for nefarious reasons. They are often used as guardians of ancient tombs, though some texts mention mummies who rose from their tombs by sheer force of will. The mummy’s most dreaded ability is their ability to infect the living with mummy rot, which can cause a victim to decompose until they are nothing more than a pile of sand and dust.

*—Creaturi Obscuria*

### Special Abilities

**Despair**

Any creature who sees a mummy must make a **Spirit save** or be paralyzed with fear for 1d4 rounds. Affected creatures cannot be targeted again by that mummy’s despair for 24 hours.

**Damage Resistance**

### Reagents and Remnants

**Cursed Wrappings** (Remnant)
The mummy’s ancient, brittle wrappings retain a trace of the curse that sustains it. When burned, they release a foul smoke that causes nausea and dread. Alchemists can distill them into powders for curse-binding rituals or to strengthen protective wards.
**Value:** 300 gp
**Harvesting Difficulty:** Difficult

**Preserved Organ** (Reagent)
A blackened, resin-soaked organ can sometimes be extracted from the mummy’s embalmed body. These are highly prized by necromancers, as they can be used to empower undead summoning or strengthen curses.
**Value:** 450 gp
**Harvesting Difficulty:** Very Difficult

**Canopic Organ Vessel** (Reagent)
