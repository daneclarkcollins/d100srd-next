The small creature in front of you stares up with piercing black eyes. The pallid lips of its purplish corpse-like mouth stretch back to show large yellowed teeth, as it begins a shrill scream that would cause the strongest warrior to cringe.

A lakava is a horror to behold. It is a grotesque thing, the likes of which has even caused me terror and disgust I never thought possible. It is a malevolent spirit from the Navirim, whose entrance into our world is a particularly awful chosen vessel to possess: the body of an infant or small child who has passed. The lakava changes the body in terrible ways, as if the innocent vessel is trying to reject such an abomination. Just to behold one, or hear its cry, is enough to shake a person's grip on reality. I know this all too well, and would as soon forget I did.

—Etahn

### Special Abilities

**Withering Attacks**  
All of a lakava's natural attacks deal normal damage **plus 1d4 points of Wither damage**, which bypasses Armor Value unless the target's armor is silvered. Wither manifests as cracking skin, dark veins, and unnatural decay.

**Maniacal Laugh**  
As a free action, the lakava emits a shrill laugh. One target within hearing range must succeed on a **Spirit save** or become *Shaken*. Each failure also triggers a Sanity check (0/1d2 Horror).
