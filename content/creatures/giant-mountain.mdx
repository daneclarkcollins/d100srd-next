Mountain giants are larger than their hill cousins, and far more antisocial. They spend most of their days alone in the high peaks of the mountains, allowing them the solitude they seek, coming down only in the summer to hunt. A hunting mountain giant can devastate a region as they seek enough food for the year to come, and all living creatures are quite literally fair game, even their hill giant cousins. They keep their food preserved in cold caves high in the peaks where they live.

They mate infrequently, perhaps only once a century if my calculations are correct, so a mountain giant might only mate two to three times in its 300 year lifespan. Preferring solitude above all other things, the mating couple goes their separate ways after they consort, with the female giant raising the offspring. The rate of infanticide in mountain giants is high—they are as likely to be killed by their own mother in a fit of temper as they are another mountain giant for food—with only half surviving to adolescence.

A mountain giant shows great resistance to magic, which may stem from their gray, stone-like skin. The largest of all giants, they typically stand 22 feet tall and weigh around 12,000 lbs.
