Root golems are a strange thing to behold. While most golems are made of inanimate (or dead) materials, the root golem seems to be composed of living material. Root golems are always found underground, which I suppose is natural for them. The mages who created them must have needed to create a golem, been in a cave, and thought, "eh, roots will do!"

Root golems pack a wallop when they hit you, and make sure they dont get their long fingers around your neck! Fire is the best way to put them down. That, and a sharp axe.

—Etahn

### Special Abilities

**Grab**  
When the root golem hits with an attack that includes Grab, it and the target roll a Heroic Action. If the target loses, it is grappled and cannot move or act except to escape. Escaping requires a successful Heroic Action on their turn.

**Strangle**  
If a grappled opponent fails to break free, the root golem may deal 1d4+1+1d4 damage as a free action by succeeding on a Heroic Action (opposed as per Grab).

**Fire Vulnerability**  
Fire does double damage to a root golem.

**Water Source**  
A root golem loses 5 HP per day without access to a water source. While in contact with water, it heals 5 HP per round.

**Construct Traits**  
Immune to disease, death effects, mind-affecting effects, paralysis, poison, sleep, stun, and any effect requiring a Fortitude save.
