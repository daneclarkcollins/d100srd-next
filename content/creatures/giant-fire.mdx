Fire giants are so rare most believe them to be mere myth. There is some archeological evidence that indicates creatures of their description did exist, living very solitary lives much like their mountain giant cousins. Fire giant ruins exhibit little evidence of food storage, and myths say that they had little need for sustenance. Further research, of course, would be needed to determine how such a large creature could sustain itself without the standard nutrition most living beings need—it is quite possible some form of elemental magic might be at the source of their ability to survive without food as we know it.

Fire giants worship fire, and tales from the dwarves hold that fire giants brought fire to their own civilization. This might indicate that fire giants are more civil and intelligent than their cousins, which also aligns with accounts that they avoided association of any kind with them as well.

They have a great resistance to magic and heat, though have a vulnerability to cold.

As best as I can reckon, fire giants had (or have) a lifespan of around 2,000 years. They have gray skin and fire-like hair, and stand 12 feet tall, weighing about 1,400 lbs.
